I am currently in the final year of my PhD in the field of renewable energy systems at the EPFL Laboratory of Photovoltaics and Thin Film Electronics (PV Lab). My research focuses on the use of electric vehicle batteries as short-term storage for solar photovoltaic electricity, also known as vehicle to grid (V2G). This process could enable us to integrate more renewable electricity into our electricity consumption, while providing clean electricity for charging electric vehicles. Before joining PV-lab, I spent two years in Japan, studying in the laboratory of Pr. Naoki Takano in the Department of Mechanical Engineering and obtained a Master's Degree in Engineering from Keio University, Japan. I started my engineering studies at the Ecole Centrale de Nantes in France. During my studies in Nantes, I worked on the design of cables for offshore wind turbines.

Curent Project

I am involved in the development of the citiwatts tool, an online geographical platform for energy planning. I am implementing the models developed in my research as calculation modules to make them accessible to everyone. The tool is available for all of Europe, don't hesitate to try out your charging scenario!

Latest Publication

Our article "A Spatiotemporal analysis of photovoltaic electricity storage potential in electric vehicles" was published in June 2025 in the International Journal of Sustaible Energy Planning and Managment. The article presents the latest version of our methodology to quantify the storage potential of photovoltaic electricity in electric vehicule batteries. The maethodology is based on a geospacial modelling of the mobility needs, scenarios of charging behaviours and typical meteorological data of solar electricity production potential.
